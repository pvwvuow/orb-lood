How to read this page: every section has a status pill (ok, warn, fail). The most useful single line is the verdict under each ICE server: relay candidate obtained means TURN is reachable AND your credentials are accepted; no relay means either the TURN port is blocked from this network, the username/password don't match coturn's /etc/turnserver.conf, or coturn's external-ip setting is wrong. The live log records every probe with timestamps so you can paste it into a bug report.

Common Iran-network failures: UDP often blocked at the carrier → expect turn:…:3478 via UDP to fail; turn:…:443?transport=tcp and turns:…:443 should still work because TLS-on-443 is indistinguishable from HTTPS. If none of the TURN URLs return a relay candidate, the CDN in front of the domain is probably eating the TURN traffic — point a separate subdomain (e.g. turn.orblood.ir) directly at the VPS and use that as TURN_HOST.
